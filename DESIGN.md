## Brand & Style

The TRMS System relies on a clean, robust, and highly functional aesthetic. It focuses on clarity, productivity, and accessibility, reflecting a "Modern Corporate" style. The design language avoids unnecessary ornament in favor of strong visual hierarchies, precise alignment, and accessible contrast ratios.

The color palette leverages the highly perceptive `oklch` color space to ensure uniform brightness and contrast across all hues. The interfaces feel open and uncluttered, prioritizing data visibility and intuitive user actions.

## Colors

The color system uses semantic tokens to abstract the exact hex or RGB values away from implementation. It relies entirely on the `oklch` color space, which offers perceptually uniform lightness and more natural hue shifts than standard RGB or HSL.

- **Backgrounds & Surfaces**: The light mode relies on a stark white `{colors.background}` (`oklch(1 0 0)`) paired with slightly off-white and cool grey cards (`oklch(0.9784 0.0011 197.1387)`), creating subtle differentiation between the page canvas and its content containers.
- **Primary Actions**: A striking, vivid indigo (`oklch(0.6723 0.1606 244.9955)`) functions as the primary accent. It offers high contrast against both light and dark backgrounds.
- **State Colors**: Destructive actions are marked with a deep red (`oklch(0.6188 0.2376 25.7658)`), while warnings and success states use vibrant, instantly recognizable hues (`#f59e0b` and `#16a34a`).
- **Dark Mode Strategy**: The dark mode is not a simple inversion. Backgrounds drop to true black (`oklch(0 0 0)`), while cards and surfaces adopt deep, cool navy tones (`oklch(0.2097 0.008 274.5332)`). The primary accent color retains almost identical luminance (`oklch(0.6692 0.1607 245.011)`) to ensure brand consistency regardless of the theme.

## Typography

The typographical system emphasizes legibility and clean lines. While the underlying fonts fallback to familiar system defaults like Open Sans and Georgia, the primary font across all contexts (sans, serif, and mono) is forcefully overridden to use **Kanit**.

Kanit is a modern, geometric sans-serif that supports both Latin and Thai scripts beautifully, making it ideal for internationalized interfaces that need to feel unified.

- The typography avoids extreme weights, relying on structured hierarchies through size and semantic color application (e.g., distinguishing `{colors.foreground}` from `{colors.muted-foreground}`).

## Layout & Spacing

Spacing is derived from a strict 4px (0.25rem) base scale (`{spacing.base}`). This ensures mathematically sound rhythm across margins, paddings, and gaps.

- Components are spaced predictably to maintain a comfortable density, suitable for dense data applications like dashboards and tables, without feeling cramped.

## Elevation & Depth

The system eschews heavy drop shadows in favor of extreme subtlety or total flatness.

- **Shadows**: The shadow tokens (`shadow-sm`, `shadow-md`, etc.) are defined but their opacity is explicitly set to `0` or they use a completely transparent color (`hsl(202.8169 89.1213% 53.1373% / 0)`). This indicates a design intent that leans heavily into "flat design".
- **Layering**: Instead of using shadows to indicate elevation, the system relies on subtle background color shifts (e.g., from `{colors.background}` to `{colors.card}`) and borders (`{colors.border}`) to define the edges of surfaces and components.

## Shapes

Shapes are moderately rounded to soften the interface without making it playful.

- **Base Radius**: The foundational radius is `7px`.
- **Variations**: Smaller elements (like badges or internal inputs) use `{rounded.sm}` (3px) and `{rounded.md}` (5px), while larger containers like cards use `{rounded.lg}` (7px). This creates a nested harmony where outer containers are slightly more rounded than their inner children.

## Components

### Cards and Surfaces

Cards form the foundational building blocks of the layout. They are visually separated from the background via a dedicated `{colors.card}` background and a subtle 1px `{colors.border}`. They employ a `{rounded.lg}` corner to distinguish them as standalone interactive or informational units.

### Buttons and Actions

Primary buttons leverage the deep indigo `{colors.primary}` with stark `{colors.primary-foreground}` text. They utilize a slightly tighter `{rounded.md}` corner, identifying them clearly as clickable primitives distinct from structural containers.

### Sidebar Layout

The application features a distinct sidebar structure with its own micro-theme. In light mode, the sidebar uses the `{colors.card}` background to blend structurally, while in dark mode, it maintains deep cool tones. The sidebar contains its own primary, accent, and border tokens to allow its visual hierarchy to be tuned independently of the main content area.
